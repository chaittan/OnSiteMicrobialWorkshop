About- Motivation,Beneficiaries, Stakeholders

Monitoring water quality of water bodies is crucial to certify its usage and maintain a healthy ecosystem. It can be used to certify the quality of discharge from wastewater treatment plants, industrial affluents, and commercial affluents including hospitals and households. In the region of Jammu and Kashmir, there are over a dozen rivers which are source of irrigation, fishing and drinking water for a large part of the society. According to report published in 2022 by National Water Monitoring Program (NWMP), established by the Central Pollution Control Board (CPCB), shows that 8 river stretches/locations have been identified as polluted based on Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD) measurements. High BOD levels in water bodies can indicate the presence of pollutants such as sewage, industrial waste, or excessive nutrients. These pollutants can deplete the oxygen levels in water, causing harm to aquatic organisms and disrupting the ecosystem.

A major limitation of such a report is that it is based on a single metric, in this case, the BOD. Microbial diagnostics and water chemistry was not performed to look at other sources of pollutants and harmful pathogens present in water. Microbial profiling through metagenomics can help in source tracking and monitoring rising antimicrobial resistance (AMR) and can help manage and prevent potential health outbreaks as was the case with COVID. Molecular profiling can help state water bodies, government agencies and policymakers diversify their approach towards wastewater treatment through novel state-of-the-art methods. It can help in monitoring the usage fertilizers in agriculture, antibiotics in the society to prevent rise in AMR and soundly plan the placements of treatment plants at locations of severe pollution. Molecular diagnostics workshop aims to introduce and explore this process, for the first time, in the state of Jammu and Kashmir. It will help train manpower that can perform wet lab experiments including water chemistry, extraction of DNA material, PCR, genome sequencing and bioinformatics analysis. This workshop will pave path to establish a molecular diagnostics laboratory at IIT Jammu that will further the process and aim to develop novel methods of molecular bioinformatics and AMR surveillance in order to help the state of Jammu and Kashmir to form policies backed by data-analysis to improve the quality of water bodies across the state.

Lab in a suitcase

There are two scenarios that will be realized during the workshop and both of them will cover the entire workflow as shown in Fig. 1. First scenario involves bringing the samples collected from different sites back to the laboratory while in the second scenario, we perform the entire workflow on-site using the envisaged lab-in-a-suitcase. This kit was designed and assembled by Prof. David Werner and his group at Newcastle University. One day of this workshop will include on-site visit with the proposed kit and conducting the entire workflow in-situ.

About IIT Jammu


Central Instrumentation Facility (CIF) named SAPTARSHI at IIT Jammu houses a wide array of high-end instruments for pushing the boundaries of research in science and technology to a higher level. The SAPTARSHI lab is a conglomeration of seven labs, namely; Microscopy Lab, Spectroscopy Lab, X-ray Based Characterization Lab, optics and chromatography lab, Nanofabrication and Physical Property Characterization lab (NPPCL), Thermal Analysis Lab (TAL), and Water Quality Testing Lab (WQTL). This is the heart of the institution, which helps the faculties, research scholars, and students to carry out globally competitive research in basic, applied, and medical sciences and publish their research findings in peer-reviewed high-impact factor journals for a sustainable society.
